# Comparison Post Template

## Objective
Compare two approaches, methods, or tools to help the audience make informed decisions. Never bash competitors.

## Hook Styles
- "{Approach A} vs {Approach B}: Which is right for {audience}?"
- "Most people choose {common approach}. Here's when {alternative} works better."
- "We evaluated {options} for {use case}. Here's what we found."

## Structure
1. **Frame the comparison**: What two things are being compared and why
2. **Approach A**: Strengths and when it fits
3. **Approach B**: Strengths and when it fits
4. **Our take**: What we chose and why (honest reasoning)
5. **CTA**: What should the reader consider for their situation

## Rules
- NEVER bash competitors by name without L3 approval
- Compare approaches/methods, not specific company products (unless educational)
- Be fair — show genuine pros of both sides
- Base comparisons on facts, not opinions dressed as facts
- If comparing to InBharat products, disclose the conflict of interest

## Image Style
- Side-by-side comparison table
- Pros/cons grid
- Decision tree diagram
